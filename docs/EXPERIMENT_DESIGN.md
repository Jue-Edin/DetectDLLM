# Experiment design

## Goal

Detect whether a text is human-written or generated by the local DUO DLLM.

## Data

1. Start from a human corpus in `data/human/`.
2. Split it into train/val/test by hashing `source_id` with a fixed seed.
3. For every human passage, generate one DUO passage with matched target length.
4. Evaluate detectors on the resulting balanced binary dataset.

## Main method: analytic DUO detector

For a text `X0`:

1. Randomly mask a fraction of tokens to form a one-step forward surrogate `X1'`.
2. Ask DUO for the reverse conditional logits at the masked positions.
3. Compute the observed masked-token log-probability of the original text.
4. Compute the conditional mean and variance analytically from the same DUO logits.
5. Form the normalized score
   `z = (observed - mean) / sqrt(var + eps)`.

## Baselines

- `baseline`: plain mean masked-token log-probability under DUO.
- `fastdetectgpt_surrogate`: Fast-DetectGPT baseline using a local or online AR model.

## Validation protocol

- Choose score orientation on validation only.
- Choose threshold on validation only.
- Choose the best mask ratio for the analytic DUO detector using validation ROC-AUC only.
- Report the final metrics on test.

## Metrics

- ROC-AUC
- PR-AUC
- Accuracy
- Balanced accuracy
- TPR@1%FPR
- TPR@5%FPR
- TPR@10%FPR
